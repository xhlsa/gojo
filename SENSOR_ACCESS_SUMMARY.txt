================================================================================
ARCHITECTURAL BOTTLENECK ANALYSIS
================================================================================

Why is Termux:API capped at 15 Hz?

Android Sensor HAL
    ↓ (hardware can deliver 50-200+ Hz)
Termux:API SensorAPI Service
    ↓ (service can handle full rate)
LocalSocket IPC Channel
    ↓ (BOTTLENECK HERE: ~15-20 Hz capacity)
Termux Terminal Process
    ↓ (can handle higher rates)

The LocalSocket communication protocol is optimized for request-response
interactions, not high-frequency streaming. This is by design - native Android
apps use memory-mapped sensor buffers or direct HAL access instead.

Termux:API trades performance for accessibility and sandboxing.

================================================================================
PERFORMANCE REALITY CHECK
================================================================================

Is 15 Hz enough for motion tracking?

Event Type              Duration      Minimum Freq   15 Hz Sufficient?
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Hard braking            1-2 sec       >5 Hz          ✓ Yes
Impact/collision        100-500ms     >10 Hz         ✓ Yes
Swerving                500-1000ms    >5 Hz          ✓ Yes
Lane departure          500ms-2sec    >5 Hz          ✓ Yes
Pothole/bump            100-200ms     >10 Hz         ✓ Yes (barely)
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Verdict: 15 Hz is sufficient for safety incident detection.

NOT sufficient for:
- Vibration analysis (needs 50+ Hz)
- Shock peak capture (needs 100+ Hz)
- Gyro calibration (needs >50 Hz for better accel lock)
